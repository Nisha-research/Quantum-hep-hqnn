EDIT 3: Add clarification to Section 3A (Training Time)

After the table in Section 3A, add this paragraph:

Training Time Analysis:
The 36.2× training time increase for HQNN vs. CNN is due to parameter-shift 
gradient computation required by PennyLane 0.45's removal of differentiable 
quantum layers (see Section 4, implementation note). Each of the 24 quantum 
parameters requires 2 circuit evaluations per training step to estimate gradients, 
compared to single forward-backward pass for classical layers. This overhead is 
expected and scales linearly with qubit count; at 4 qubits it remains tractable 
(~235s for 140 samples). Deeper circuits or larger datasets would require 
distributed training or hardware-in-the-loop gradient estimation, addressed in 
Phase 2 (Future Work).

Why: Explains the time ratio without making it look like the HQNN is broken.
